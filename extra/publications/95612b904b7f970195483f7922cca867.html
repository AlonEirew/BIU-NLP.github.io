Description
Effectively consuming information from large amounts of texts, which are often largely redundant in their content, is an old but increasingly pressing challenge. It is well illustrated by the perpetual attempts to move away from the flat result lists of search engines towards more structured fact-based presentations. Some recent attempts at this challenge are based on presenting structured information that was formulated according to pre-defined knowledge schemes, such as Freebase and Google’s knowledge graph. We propose an alternative, as well as complementary, approach that attempts to consolidate and structure all textual statements in a document collection based on the inference relations between them.
